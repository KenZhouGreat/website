Organisations large and small welcome feedback from their customers, and many of them invite feedback via their websites. Anyone who has used a computer to complain via a website knows it can be a frustrating and time-consuming experience. It’s even harder from a mobile device.

A lot of complaints go un-logged because of these obstacles.

We think it’s time to put consumers in control.

Post-A-Vox is a comfortable, straightforward and graceful way to complain from a mobile. Not just to one organisation, but any organisation. No lumps.

Andrew Taylor

Andrew is a co-founder of Post-A-Vox and has spent years advising government agencies and household names on how to make it easy for people to complain.

As a career technology entrepreneur, he founded both the Satisfy and CaseWork businesses, which continue to provide complaint-handling technology to enterprise customers in Australia.

He’s a Cambridge graduate who clears his head on the yoga mat (he’s working towards certification as an Iyengar teacher) and whenever possible likes to retreat to his off-the-grid country hideout.

Aija Neilands

Aija designs consumer interfaces for Post-A-Vox. Before this, working with Andrew’s team, she was immersed in complaints data for enterprise clients.

Before that, she was putting her clear thinking and straight talking to good use as a member of an award-winning creative team in a global ad agency.

What she really likes is making things and helping good things happen (such as Crochet Coral Reef).

Khalid Hameed

Khalid is Post-A-Vox’s technical architect, and a co-founder. He and Andrew have worked together since 2007, building Web-based complaint-handling systems for major enterprise clients.

He cut his technical teeth in Pakistan working on enterprise systems in the multinational resources sector. He has participated in telecommunications and internet access start-ups in Pakistan and Australia.

His home-grown zucchinis are the talk of the office and when he’s at home, he spends a lot of time messing around making things. He speaks Urdu, Punjabi and English.
